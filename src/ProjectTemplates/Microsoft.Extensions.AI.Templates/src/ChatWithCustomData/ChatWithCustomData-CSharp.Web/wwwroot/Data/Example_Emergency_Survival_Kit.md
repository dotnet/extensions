## 7. Water PurificationIn case of an emergency situation where clean water may not be readily available, Life

Guard X's Emergency Survival Kit includes a water purification system to ensure access to safe drinking water. The kit contains a portable water filter with a high-performance filtration membrane that removes 99.9999% of waterborne bacteria and 99.9% of waterborne protozoan parasites. This allows you to safely drink from natural water sources such as rivers and lakes. Additionally, the kit includes water purification tablets for an extra layer of protection. Always follow the instructions provided with the water purification system for proper use.

### 7.1 Usage

To use the water purification system, locate the portable water filter in the kit. Extend the tubing and securely attach it to the water source, ensuring a tight seal. Begin to pump the handle to force water through the filtration membrane, creating clean drinking water. To use the water purification tablets, simply follow the instructions provided in the kit and place the appropriate number of tablets into the water to be treated. Wait for the recommended time before consuming the water.

### 7.2 Maintenance

After each use, thoroughly clean the portable water filter and allow it to dry completely before storing it back in the kit. Always inspect the filtration membrane for any damage or signs of wear, and replace it if necessary. Ensure that the water purification tablets are stored in a cool, dry place and check their expiration date regularly. Replace any expired tablets immediately.

### 7.3 Safety Precautions

When using the water purification system, be cautious of the water source and any potential contaminants. Always prioritize clear, flowing water when possible, and avoid stagnant or visibly contaminated water. If unsure of the water quality, use the water purification tablets as an additional measure. Follow all safety precautions and instructions provided with the water purification system to prevent any risk of illness from consuming contaminated water.
